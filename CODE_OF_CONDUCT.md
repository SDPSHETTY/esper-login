# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in the Esper Login project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

* **Being Professional**: Using welcoming and professional language appropriate for enterprise software
* **Being Collaborative**: Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
* **Being Constructive**: Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
* **Focusing on the Community**: What is best for the Esper community and users
* **Showing Empathy**: Being understanding towards other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Harassment, trolling, or personal attacks
* Publishing private information without permission
* Inappropriate conduct in a professional setting
* Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in an enterprise environment

## Enterprise Context

Given this project's focus on enterprise login automation:

### Security Standards
* **Credential Protection**: Never share or commit API keys or sensitive credentials
* **Access Control**: Respect tenant access boundaries and permissions
* **Data Handling**: Follow enterprise data protection standards
* **Responsible Use**: Use automation tools ethically and as intended

### Professional Standards
* **Quality Code**: Maintain high standards for code quality and documentation
* **Security First**: Consider security implications in all contributions
* **User Experience**: Focus on reliability and ease of use for enterprise users
* **Compliance**: Follow Esper platform guidelines and best practices

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project team. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated and will result in a response that is deemed necessary and appropriate to the circumstances.

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

## Contact

For questions about this Code of Conduct, please open an issue in the project repository.
